# INSUFFICIENT BASIS

> When "we do not know" is the answer.

## When to Use

When the team cannot reach quorum on any recommendation, when the evidence is genuinely insufficient, the synthesis should say so clearly rather than hedging its way to a low-confidence recommendation.

"We do not know" is a finding. It may be the most important finding.

## Template

```markdown
INSUFFICIENT BASIS

The team has investigated [topic] and cannot recommend a course of action.

**What we found:** [summary of findings]
**What we did not find:** [what remains unknown]
**What we recommend investigating before a decision is made:** [next steps]

This is not failure. This is the highest form of service — protecting the
human from acting on insufficient understanding.
```

## Why This Exists

The pressure to produce a recommendation is real. A team that has spent time and resources investigating feels compelled to produce *something*. But a low-confidence recommendation dressed in the language of analysis is more dangerous than an honest admission of insufficient knowledge.

The human needs to know what the team found, what it didn't find, and what would need to be true before a recommendation could be made. This is the output that serves them best when certainty is not available.
